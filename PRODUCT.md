# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Policyholders** — individuals who want to understand, compare, and optimize their insurance policies without wading through dense legal language. They come to ClaimWise confused or overwhelmed and leave with clarity.

**Insurance professionals** — agents, brokers, and advisors who manage multiple client policies. They use ClaimWise to accelerate analysis, generate comparison reports, and serve clients faster.

## Product Purpose

ClaimWise transforms insurance documents into actionable intelligence. Upload a policy, get a plain-English breakdown with claim readiness scoring, compare coverage side-by-side, and ask follow-up questions through an AI chat. It exists to make insurance transparent and manageable — replacing frustration with understanding.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, Clear, Authoritative.** The voice of someone who knows insurance inside out but refuses to sound like a lawyer. No jargon, no hype, no "empowering you to." Direct sentences, confident recommendations, zero condescension. The design should feel like a well-organized desk — everything in its place, nothing loud for the sake of being loud.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS look.** No cluttered dashboards, no identical card grids with icon + heading + text, no purple-to-blue gradients, no hero-metric templates. Avoid the "built with a template" feel entirely.
- **Insurance-company clichés.** No navy-blue-and-white "trust" palettes, no shield icons everywhere, no stock photo of a handshake.
- **AI slop tells.** No side-stripe borders, no gradient text, no all-caps eyebrows above every section, no numbered section markers (01 / 02 / 03) as default scaffolding.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity first.** Every screen answers "what am I looking at, and what should I do next?" within seconds.
2. **Confidence without noise.** The interface earns trust through precision and restraint, not through decorative badges or excessive UI chrome.
3. **Data as a first-class citizen.** Policies, comparisons, and analysis results are the star. Layout serves readability, comparison, and scanability.
4. **Calm complexity.** Insurance is complex — the UI should feel simpler than the subject matter, not amplify it. Progressive disclosure, clear hierarchies, generous whitespace.
5. **Human tone, machine speed.** Copy reads like an expert colleague, not a chatbot or a legal document. Responses feel fast and definitive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard best practices. Semantic HTML, keyboard-navigable interfaces, sufficient color contrast (at least WCAG AA as a floor), reduced motion support, and readable font sizes. No specific compliance level mandated beyond practical inclusivity.
